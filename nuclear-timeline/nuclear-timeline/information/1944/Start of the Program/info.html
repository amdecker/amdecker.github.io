1944 – Start of the Program

On September 29, 1944, Igor Kurchatov, the head physicist of the Soviet nuclear program, sent a letter to M.G. Pervukhin (Deputy Chairman of the Council of People’s Commissars and a key atomic administrator) and Lavrentiy Beria in which he asks for more funding for the nuclear project. The Soviet nuclear program was extraordinarily underfunded and understaffed during World War II, especially in comparison with the United States’ Manhattan Project. During WWII, the Soviet program had ~20 physicists and a small staff, while at its peak the Manhattan Project employed a staggering 130,000 workers.
