When the Soviet Union collapsed in late 1991, 3200 strategic nuclear warheads were scattered in Ukraine, Kazakhstan, and Belarus. The Nunn-Lugar Cooperative Threat Reduction Program created by U.S. Senators Sam Nunn and Richard Lugar helped the Soviet Union gather these nuclear weapons to ensure they did not fall into the hands of terrorists or untrustworthy governments. The Nunn-Lugar Act grew to a $1 billion per year initiative. By 1994, all of the nuclear arsenals of the former Soviet states were in Russia, and the United States helped fund efforts to store nuclear materials safely and eliminate a large part of the Soviet nuclear arsenal.
